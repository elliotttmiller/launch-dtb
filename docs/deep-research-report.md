## What this means for a production-ready WooCommerce and Veeqo pipeline

WooCommerceвАЩs own shipping documentation says that many API-based shipping methods require products to have **weights and dimensions** set, and its box-packing documentation says that for **variable products**, shipping dimensions can be set **per variation**, with parent values used only as fallback when a variation has no explicit values. WooCommerceвАЩs import/export documentation also supports shipping columns for **weight**, **length**, **width**, and **height**. In other words, the correct canonical data model for your catalog is not вАЬone row per merchandised parent pageвАЭ; it is **one row per actual shippable SKU/variation**, with parent-child relationships modeled only as needed for storefront UX. оИАciteоИВturn28search0оИВturn28search10оИВturn28search13оИБ

If you are importing variable products through WooCommerceвАЩs CSV-based tooling, variation handling matters. WooCommerce documentation for variation importing states that variable parents and child variations are treated separately in the Product CSV Import Suite workflow. Even if you do not use that premium suite, the core lesson is the same: **do not flatten variant shipping data into the parent row** if the sellable variation has its own SKU or ship dimensions. Instead, put the shipping values on the variation record that is actually purchased. оИАciteоИВturn28search3оИБ

VeeqoвАЩs own materials reinforce the same design. Veeqo says its WooCommerce integration can bring WooCommerce orders into Veeqo while keeping inventory synced, and VeeqoвАЩs shipping features state that it uses product info to pre-fill package details including **weight**, **dimensions**, **HS codes**, and **origin country**. VeeqoвАЩs help documentation also says you can save **shipping weights and dimensions at the product level**, and those details are then automatically applied when shipping that product. That means the cleanest operating model is to make WooCommerce the place where your catalog and sellable variations are published, while ensuring that the shipping fields entering Veeqo are already normalized and trustworthy. оИАciteоИВturn30search0оИВturn30search1оИВturn30search13оИВturn30search16оИБ

That architecture also lowers risk for automation. If your catalog has missing or parent-only shipping data, WooCommerce live-rate and box-packing logic can degrade, and VeeqoвАЩs auto-filled package details become less reliable. If, instead, every sellable SKU row has explicit shipping metadata, Veeqo can auto-populate shipment details, WooCommerce can calculate rates and box usage more predictably, and downstream warehouse staff are less likely to hand-correct shipments at pack time. ## The production extraction standard I would use

A robust catalog-enrichment job for your environment should treat MBM storefront data as an **input source**, not as the final truth inside your operational catalog. The authoritative internal schema should include, at minimum: `brand`, `parent_product_name`, `display_product_name`, `sku`, `currency`, `current_price`, `weight_value`, `weight_unit`, `length`, `width`, `height`, `dimension_unit`, `source_url`, `source_last_verified_at`, `variant_axis_1`, `variant_value_1`, `variant_axis_2`, `variant_value_2`, and `shipping_data_status`. This recommendation is an inference from the way MBM exposes variants and from WooCommerce/VeeqoвАЩs dependence on product-level shipping metadata. оИАciteоИВturn23view0оИВturn23view1оИВturn27view0оИВturn28search10оИВturn30search13оИБ

For extraction logic, I would use four passes. First, collect the brand inventory counts and seed URLs from the brand pages. Second, discover product URLs and variants from product cards, in-page variant links, and product-family pages. Third, open each product in USD mode and extract `name`, `SKU`, `USD price`, and `Additional information` values. Fourth, normalize every result into a one-row-per-SKU catalog table and perform assertions such as: all non-virtual SKUs must have weight and dimensions; all rows must have stable source URLs; and any multi-option product must prove whether its page exposes one SKU or multiple sellable SKUs. The need for this approach follows directly from the MBM storefront patterns verified in this session. оИАciteоИВturn2view2оИВturn2view3оИВturn23view0оИВturn23view1оИВturn27view0оИБ

For WooCommerce import, the safest route is to load native shipping fields rather than storing shipping data only in custom meta, because WooCommerceвАЩs documented shipping behavior and import/export logic are built around the native product shipping fields. For Veeqo, the reconciliation rule should be strict: if WooCommerce has a variation-level weight/dimensions record and Veeqo has something different for the same SKU, flag it as an exception rather than silently overwriting. ## Coverage limits in this session

I was able to verify that MBM exposes the required data and I extracted a directly confirmed subset, but I was **not able to guarantee a complete scrape of every single Level 5 and Columbia product/variant in this browsing environment**. The reason is not that the data is absent; it is that the brand-page pagination did not behave deterministically here, and the site mixes several product modeling patterns, including page-per-variation and configurable sets with linked options.
