# Limited Liability Company Operating Agreement of Drywall Toolbox LLC

This Limited Liability Company Operating Agreement, including all schedules and exhibits attached hereto, is entered into and made effective as of **[Effective Date]** by and among the undersigned members identified in **Schedule A**.

The Members hereby agree as follows.

---

## Article 1 — Definitions

For purposes of this Agreement, the following terms have the meanings set forth below. Capitalized terms not defined in this Agreement have the meanings given under the Minnesota Revised Uniform Limited Liability Company Act, Minn. Stat. Chapter 322C.

1. **Act** means the Minnesota Revised Uniform Limited Liability Company Act, Minn. Stat. Chapter 322C, as amended from time to time.
2. **Agreement** means this Limited Liability Company Operating Agreement of Drywall Toolbox LLC, including all schedules, exhibits, amendments, and written consents adopted under this Agreement.
3. **Company** means Drywall Toolbox LLC, a Minnesota limited liability company.
4. **Company Property** means all assets owned, licensed, controlled, created for, purchased by, assigned to, or used by the Company, including inventory, cash, accounts, receivables, intellectual property, domains, source code, repositories, product data, customer data, repair records, supplier records, pricing models, schematics, brand assets, credentials, integrations, and operating systems.
5. **Economic Percentage** means the percentage used for profits, losses, distributions, and liquidation proceeds, subject to tax and capital-account rules in this Agreement.
6. **Member** means each person admitted as a member of the Company under this Agreement and the Act.
7. **Membership Interest** means a Member's complete ownership interest in the Company, including governance rights, voting rights, information rights, economic rights, and any transferable interest.
8. **Ordinary Course** means routine Company activity consistent with the approved budget, approved operating plan, and historical business practice.
9. **Reserved Matter** means a decision requiring unanimous Member approval under Article 7.
10. **Transferable Interest** means the right to receive distributions from the Company as provided by the Act and this Agreement, separate from governance or management rights.
11. **Voting Percentage** means the percentage used to determine voting power for Member approvals.

## Article 3 — Business Purpose and Scope

### 3.1 Business Purpose

The Company may engage in any lawful business permitted under Minnesota law, including operating a contractor-focused ecommerce, services, repair, parts, schematics, catalog, media, fulfillment, and technology platform for drywall tools, parts, accessories, consumables, and related services.

### 3.2 Drywall Toolbox Operating Model

The Members acknowledge that Drywall Toolbox is intended to operate as a headless commerce platform and operational business system that may include:

1. ecommerce catalog browsing, search, product detail pages, variation selection, cart, checkout, and order confirmation;
2. WooCommerce-backed product, order, customer, and payment workflows;
3. drywall tool, replacement-part, and accessory sales;
4. schematics-driven parts discovery and part lookup workflows;
5. repair intake, repair quoting, repair status tracking, warranty evaluation, and repair-order workflows;
6. rewards, retention, account, and customer-service experiences;
7. catalog, taxonomy, image, media, supplier, SKU, pricing, and product-data operations;
8. integrations with payment processors, shipping tools, fulfillment tools, inventory systems, Veeqo, QuickBooks, tax tools, analytics systems, advertising platforms, hosting providers, and related systems; and
9. any other related or adjacent activity approved by the Members.

### 3.3 Authority to Evolve Business

The Members intend for the Company to adapt its product catalog, supplier relationships, technology stack, fulfillment model, service offerings, and operational workflows over time. No amendment is required merely because the Company expands from ecommerce into repairs, schematics, services, wholesale, supplier operations, marketplace operations, contractor services, digital tools, or other lawful adjacent business activities.

### 4.1 Initial Members

The initial Members are listed in **Schedule A**.

### 4.2 Ownership and Voting

Unless amended by unanimous written consent, the Members shall hold equal governance ownership and equal voting power:

| Member | Ownership Percentage | Voting Percentage |
| --- | ---: | ---: |
| [Member A Name] | 50% | 50% |

### 4.3 Economic Split

Notwithstanding equal Ownership Percentages and equal Voting Percentages, the Members agree to a non-pro-rata economic arrangement:

| Member | Economic Percentage |
| --- | ---: |
| [Member A Name] | 35% |
| [Member B Name] | 65% |

Unless otherwise provided in this Agreement, profits, losses, tax allocations, operating distributions, and liquidation distributions shall be allocated according to the Economic Percentages.

### 4.4 Capital Contributions

Each Member's initial and additional capital contributions are listed in **Schedule B**. Contributions may include cash, property, inventory, equipment, intellectual property, software, domains, supplier relationships, unreimbursed startup expenses, services, or other assets if unanimously approved and documented in writing.

### 4.5 No Implied Obligation to Contribute More Capital

No Member is required to make additional capital contributions unless unanimously agreed in writing. A Member's failure to voluntarily contribute additional capital shall not reduce such Member's Membership Interest unless this Agreement is amended by unanimous written consent.

### 4.6 Treatment of Loans

A Member may loan money to the Company only with unanimous written approval. A Member loan shall not increase the lending Member's ownership, voting power, or Economic Percentage unless separately approved in a written amendment.

### 4.7 No Interest on Capital

No Member shall receive interest on capital contributions unless unanimously approved in writing.

### 4.8 No Withdrawal of Capital

No Member may withdraw capital or demand return of a contribution except as expressly provided in this Agreement or required by law.

## Article 5 — Tax, Accounting, and Capital Accounts

### 5.1 Tax Classification

Unless otherwise unanimously approved, the Company shall be treated as a partnership for federal and state income tax purposes so long as it has more than one Member.

### 5.2 Capital Accounts

The Company shall maintain capital accounts for each Member in accordance with applicable federal income tax principles, including Treasury Regulation Section 1.704-1(b), to the extent applicable.

### 5.3 Special Allocations

The Members intend that the 35% / 65% economic arrangement reflect their economic bargain. The Company shall allocate profits, losses, deductions, credits, and other tax items in a manner consistent with the Members' Economic Percentages, capital-account maintenance rules, and applicable tax law, unless the Company's tax professional determines that a different allocation is required to comply with applicable law.

### 5.4 Tax Matters Representative

The Members shall designate a partnership representative or tax matters representative for federal and state tax purposes by unanimous written consent. Until designated, **[Member Name]** shall serve in that capacity.

### 5.5 Fiscal Year

The Company's fiscal year shall be the calendar year unless otherwise required by law or unanimously approved in writing.

### 5.6 Tax Returns and Reports

The Company shall cause all required federal, state, and local tax returns and reports to be prepared and filed. Each Member shall receive the tax information reasonably necessary to prepare the Member's personal tax returns.

### 5.7 Tax Distributions

Subject to Article 10 and applicable law, the Company may make tax distributions to Members in proportion to their allocated taxable income to help satisfy tax liabilities arising from Company income. Tax distributions shall be treated as advances against ordinary distributions unless otherwise unanimously approved.

### 5.8 Withholding

The Company may withhold and pay taxes required on behalf of any Member. Amounts withheld shall be treated as distributed to that Member.

## Article 6 — Management Structure and Authority

### 6.1 Member-Managed Company

The Company shall be member-managed. Each Member shall have authority to participate in the management and conduct of the Company's business, subject to this Agreement.

### 6.2 Equal Governance Rights

Each Member shall have equal management rights and equal voting power, subject to Reserved Matters and authority limits in this Agreement.

### 6.3 Ordinary-Course Authority

Either Member may bind the Company in the Ordinary Course if the action:

1. is consistent with the approved budget or operating plan;
2. does not exceed the dollar thresholds in **Schedule D**;
3. does not involve a Reserved Matter;
4. does not create debt, lien, equity issuance, sale of material assets, personal guarantee, or long-term obligation outside approved limits;
5. does not violate law, platform policies, vendor agreements, or this Agreement; and
6. is reasonably documented in Company records.

### 6.4 No Authority Outside Agreement

No Member may bind the Company outside the scope of this Agreement. A Member acting without authority shall indemnify the Company and the other Member from resulting losses, liabilities, costs, and expenses.

### 6.5 Delegation

The Members may delegate operational tasks to employees, contractors, vendors, accountants, bookkeepers, attorneys, developers, fulfillment providers, repair technicians, agencies, or other service providers. Delegation does not relieve the Members of oversight duties.

### 6.6 Titles

The Members may use officer-style titles for operational convenience, including Chief Executive Officer, Chief Operating Officer, Chief Technology Officer, President, Treasurer, Secretary, or similar titles. Titles do not alter voting rights, ownership, fiduciary obligations, or economic rights unless unanimously approved in writing.

The following actions require unanimous written approval of all Members:

1. amending this Agreement or the Articles of Organization;
2. admitting a new Member;
3. issuing equity, options, warrants, profit interests, convertible instruments, phantom equity, or other ownership-linked rights;
4. changing Ownership Percentages, Voting Percentages, or Economic Percentages;
5. approving a merger, conversion, domestication, sale of substantially all assets, or dissolution;
6. incurring debt, credit facilities, leases, financing obligations, or personal guarantees above the threshold in Schedule D;
7. selling, transferring, pledging, licensing exclusively, or encumbering material Company Property;
8. selling or transferring domains, source-code repositories, brand assets, customer lists, catalog data, product-data assets, schematics data, or operational systems;
9. entering into supplier, platform, marketplace, fulfillment, software, agency, repair-service, hosting, payment, or integration contracts above the threshold in Schedule D;
10. entering into any related-party transaction;
11. hiring employees or contractors above the compensation threshold in Schedule D;
12. setting or materially changing Member compensation;
13. approving annual budgets and material budget revisions;
14. making distributions outside the ordinary policy in Article 10;
15. acquiring another business or forming a subsidiary;
16. filing bankruptcy, assignment for benefit of creditors, or similar insolvency action;
17. changing the Company's tax classification;
18. opening, closing, or changing control over Company bank accounts, payment processor accounts, GitHub repositories, hosting accounts, domain registrar accounts, analytics accounts, advertising accounts, marketplace accounts, or accounting systems;
19. adopting, modifying, or waiving the buy-sell, deadlock, or transfer restrictions;
20. settling litigation, audit disputes, supplier disputes, customer disputes, IP claims, chargeback disputes, or regulatory matters above the threshold in Schedule D; and
21. any other action designated as a Reserved Matter in this Agreement.

## Article 8 — Standards of Conduct, Fiduciary Duties, and Conflicts

### 8.1 Duty of Care

Each Member shall discharge duties with the care that a person in a like position would reasonably exercise under similar circumstances.

### 8.2 Duty of Loyalty

Each Member shall act in good faith and in a manner reasonably believed to be in the best interests of the Company. Without unanimous written consent, no Member may:

1. divert Company opportunities;
2. use Company Property for non-Company purposes;
3. compete with the Company while a Member, except as expressly approved;
4. misappropriate supplier, customer, catalog, repair, pricing, inventory, product, or platform data;
5. transact with the Company on unfair terms;
6. interfere with supplier, customer, contractor, employee, platform, vendor, or payment relationships;
7. access, copy, export, or use Company systems or data for personal benefit or a competing venture; or
8. conceal material business information from the Company or the other Member.

### 8.3 Conflicts of Interest

A Member with an actual or potential conflict of interest shall promptly disclose the conflict in writing. Any related-party transaction requires unanimous written approval after full disclosure.

### 8.4 Company Opportunities

Any opportunity reasonably related to drywall tools, drywall parts, automatic taping tools, finishing tools, repair services, schematics-driven part discovery, ecommerce operations, supplier relationships, product catalog operations, or the Company's platform business shall be a Company opportunity unless unanimously released in writing.

### 8.5 No Unlawful Restrictive Covenant

Nothing in this Agreement is intended to create an unlawful employment non-compete or restriction prohibited by applicable law. The Members intend only to protect Company Property, confidential information, fiduciary duties, customer relationships, supplier relationships, and legitimate business interests to the maximum extent permitted by law.

## Article 9 — Company Property, IP, Data, and Platform Assets

### 9.1 Company Ownership

All Company Property is owned by the Company, regardless of which Member created, purchased, configured, uploaded, authored, administered, funded, or maintained the asset, unless expressly excluded in **Schedule C**.

### 9.2 Covered Assets

Company Property includes, without limitation:

1. domain names, DNS records, hosting accounts, CDN configurations, SSL certificates, email accounts, and registrar accounts;
2. GitHub repositories, source code, scripts, deployment workflows, CI/CD configurations, build artifacts, technical documentation, and issue/PR history;
3. WordPress, WooCommerce, mu-plugin, frontend, backend, API, catalog, schematic, repair, reward, and integration code;
4. databases, backups, logs, exports, CSV files, JSON files, manifests, catalog policies, taxonomy rules, product mappings, SKU normalization rules, and image manifests;
5. product descriptions, pricing data, parts data, brand mappings, tool-family mappings, repair-service data, customer records, order records, quotes, invoices, returns, chargebacks, rewards data, and account history;
6. schematics, part diagrams, repair intake forms, repair workflow definitions, status maps, service packages, repair photos, and warranty-evaluation records;
7. brand names, logos, trade dress, copy, marketing creative, advertising accounts, analytics data, social media accounts, and email marketing lists;

9. payment processor, shipping, fulfillment, Veeqo, QuickBooks, tax, analytics, advertising, email, SMS, and customer-service accounts;
10. credentials, API keys, secrets, private keys, webhooks, OAuth applications, and integration tokens; and

### 9.3 Assignment

Each Member hereby assigns to the Company all right, title, and interest in Company Property created, developed, purchased, configured, or contributed by that Member for Company use, except assets expressly excluded in Schedule C.

### 9.4 Work Made for Company

All work product created by a Member, employee, contractor, developer, designer, agency, consultant, or vendor for the Company shall be Company Property to the maximum extent permitted by law and contract. The Company shall use written contractor or vendor agreements requiring assignment of intellectual property when commercially reasonable.

### 9.5 Credential Control

All material platform credentials and administrative accounts shall be maintained in a Company-approved password manager, secrets manager, or secure account-control system. No Member may withhold, rotate, revoke, export, delete, disable, transfer, or weaponize credentials except for legitimate security reasons and with notice to the other Member as soon as reasonably practicable.

### 9.6 Data Security

The Company shall use commercially reasonable safeguards for customer data, payment-related data, order data, repair records, API credentials, platform access, backups, and administrative systems. Members shall not store Company secrets in plaintext, personal notes, personal repositories, unapproved devices, or insecure channels.

## Article 10 — Distributions and Working Capital

### 10.1 Distribution Policy

Subject to this Agreement and applicable law, distributions shall be made according to Economic Percentages unless unanimously approved otherwise.

### 10.2 Solvency and Working Capital Limitation

No distribution may be made if the distribution would violate Minn. Stat. § 322C.0405 or any other applicable law. The Company shall retain sufficient reserves for:

1. inventory purchases;
2. supplier invoices;
3. payroll, contractors, and service providers;
4. taxes, accounting, and professional fees;
5. payment processor holds, chargebacks, refunds, returns, and warranties;
6. shipping, fulfillment, repair-service, and replacement-part liabilities;
7. hosting, software, integration, security, and platform costs;
8. debt service and credit obligations;
9. legal disputes or threatened claims;
10. emergency operating capital; and
11. approved growth initiatives.

### 10.3 Timing

The Members may approve monthly, quarterly, annual, or ad hoc distributions. Absent unanimous approval, no Member has the right to demand a distribution.

### 10.4 Non-Cash Distributions

No non-cash distribution may be made without unanimous written approval.

### 10.5 Clawback

If a distribution is later determined to have violated applicable law or this Agreement, the recipient Member shall repay the unlawful or improper amount to the Company to the extent required by law.

### 12.1 Ecommerce Operations

The Company may operate its storefront, checkout, catalog, parts, schematics, repairs, and account surfaces through a headless architecture or any other platform architecture approved by the Members.

### 12.2 Payment Processing

Payment processor accounts shall be Company accounts. Refunds, disputes, chargebacks, holds, reserves, subscriptions, and payment credentials shall be handled according to Company policy and platform requirements.

### 13.2 Approved Compensation

### 13.3 Sweat Equity

## Article 15 — Transfers and Encumbrances

### 15.1 Transfer Restriction

No Member may sell, assign, pledge, hypothecate, encumber, gift, transfer, or otherwise dispose of all or any part of a Membership Interest or Transferable Interest without unanimous written approval, except as expressly permitted in this Agreement.

### 15.2 No Governance Rights to Transferee

A permitted transferee receives only the rights expressly approved in writing. No transferee shall become a Member, receive governance rights, access Company records, or participate in management without unanimous written consent.

### 15.3 Right of First Refusal

Before transferring any interest to a third party, a Member must first offer the interest to the Company and then to the non-transferring Member on the same material terms. Detailed procedures shall be documented in Schedule E or a written amendment.

### 15.4 Prohibited Transfers

Transfers to competitors, suppliers, creditors, hostile parties, sanctioned persons, or persons whose ownership would materially harm the Company are prohibited unless unanimously approved in writing.

### 15.5 Involuntary Transfers

If a creditor, spouse, estate, bankruptcy trustee, receiver, or other person seeks to obtain any interest in a Member's Membership Interest or Transferable Interest, the Company and the other Member shall have the option to purchase such interest under the valuation mechanism in Schedule E.

## Article 16 — Withdrawal, Dissociation, Death, Incapacity, and Buyout

### 16.1 Voluntary Withdrawal

A Member may voluntarily withdraw only with unanimous written consent or as otherwise required by the Act. Unauthorized withdrawal is a breach of this Agreement.

### 16.2 Dissociation Events

A person is dissociated as a Member upon any event stated in the Act, this Agreement, or a written amendment, including death, incapacity, bankruptcy, expulsion, unauthorized transfer, judicial order, or other event causing dissociation under Minn. Stat. § 322C.0602.

### 16.3 Effect of Dissociation

Upon dissociation, the dissociated person ceases to have management, voting, authority, agency, information, or operational rights, except rights expressly preserved by law or this Agreement. The dissociated person retains only the economic rights required by law and this Agreement until buyout, settlement, or other disposition.

### 16.4 Mandatory Transition Duties

Upon withdrawal, dissociation, removal, buyout, or termination of active involvement, the affected Member shall:

1. return Company Property;
2. transfer administrative control of Company accounts;
3. deliver credentials, documents, backups, records, and work product;
4. stop using Company Confidential Information;
5. cooperate with bank, payment, platform, vendor, domain, repository, accounting, and tax transitions;
6. preserve records and evidence relevant to Company operations;
7. not disrupt Company systems, vendors, suppliers, customers, contractors, or employees; and
8. execute reasonable documents needed to confirm Company ownership of assets.

### 16.5 Buyout

The Company or remaining Member may purchase the dissociated Member's interest under the valuation and payment terms in Schedule E. If Schedule E is incomplete, the Members shall use a mutually agreed independent business appraiser, and the purchase price shall account for debts, liabilities, working capital, IP, inventory, receivables, goodwill, tax consequences, and pending disputes.

### 16.6 Misconduct Discount

If dissociation results from fraud, theft, intentional misconduct, gross negligence, unauthorized competition, misappropriation, data misuse, credential abuse, or material breach, the purchase price may be reduced by damages, losses, professional fees, investigation costs, remediation costs, and other amounts owed to the Company, to the maximum extent permitted by law.

## Article 17 — Deadlock Resolution

### 17.1 Deadlock Defined

A deadlock exists if the Members are unable to approve a Reserved Matter or material operational decision after good-faith discussion and the failure materially impairs Company operations, strategy, compliance, cash flow, or value.

### 17.2 Good-Faith Negotiation

Upon deadlock, either Member may issue a written deadlock notice describing the issue, proposed resolution, risks, and requested decision. The Members shall meet within ten business days after notice and attempt to resolve the issue in good faith.

### 17.3 Mediation

If the deadlock is not resolved within fifteen business days after the meeting, either Member may require non-binding mediation before a mutually agreed mediator in Minnesota or by remote proceeding.

### 17.4 Interim Operating Authority

During a deadlock, either Member may take reasonable actions necessary to preserve Company assets, maintain customer service, prevent security incidents, satisfy tax or legal obligations, preserve inventory, prevent platform downtime, process ordinary refunds, and avoid material harm, provided the action does not create a Reserved Matter without approval unless emergency action is reasonably necessary.

### 17.5 Buy-Sell Trigger

If mediation does not resolve the deadlock within thirty days after mediator appointment, either Member may trigger the buy-sell procedure in Schedule E. If Schedule E is incomplete, the Members shall negotiate a written buy-sell amendment before execution of this Agreement.

### 17.6 Arbitration

Legal disputes arising under this Agreement may be submitted to binding arbitration if required by Schedule E or by unanimous written consent. Arbitration shall not prevent a court from granting emergency injunctive relief to protect Company Property, confidential information, credentials, customer data, supplier relationships, or platform operations.

## Article 18 — Dissolution and Winding Up

### 18.1 Dissolution Events

The Company shall dissolve only upon:

1. unanimous written consent of the Members;
2. sale or disposition of substantially all Company assets followed by approval to dissolve;
3. entry of a judicial dissolution order;
4. an event requiring dissolution under the Act; or
5. any other event specified in a written amendment.

### 18.2 Winding Up

Upon dissolution, the Company shall wind up its affairs in accordance with the Act and this Agreement. The winding-up process shall include collecting receivables, liquidating or distributing assets, resolving customer obligations, completing or refunding pending orders, closing repair matters, paying creditors, handling taxes, preserving records, terminating vendor relationships, and filing required documents.

### 18.3 Order of Distribution

After dissolution, Company assets shall be applied in the following order:

1. to creditors, including Members who are creditors, to the extent permitted by law;
2. to reserves for contingent, unmatured, disputed, or unknown liabilities;
3. to return unreturned capital contributions if required by tax capital-account rules or unanimously approved liquidation policy;
4. to Members according to their positive capital-account balances, if required by applicable tax law; and
5. thereafter according to Economic Percentages, unless otherwise required by law or unanimously approved in writing.

### 18.4 Preservation of Records

The Company or designated custodian shall preserve tax, accounting, customer, order, repair, IP, employment, contractor, bank, platform, and legal records for the required legal retention period.

## Article 19 — Indemnification and Limitation of Liability

### 19.1 Indemnification

To the fullest extent permitted by law, the Company shall indemnify each Member for actions taken in good faith on behalf of the Company and within authorized authority, except for fraud, willful misconduct, knowing violation of law, bad faith, unauthorized self-dealing, gross negligence, breach of fiduciary duty, misappropriation, or material breach of this Agreement.

### 19.2 Advancement of Expenses

The Company may advance legal expenses to a Member if unanimously approved or if required by law, subject to a written undertaking to repay amounts if indemnification is ultimately unavailable.

### 19.3 Insurance

The Company may obtain general liability, cyber liability, product liability, errors and omissions, workers' compensation, property, auto, key-person, D&O/E&O, or other insurance approved by the Members.

### 19.4 Limitation of Liability

No Member shall be liable to the Company or another Member for good-faith business decisions made within authorized authority, except to the extent liability arises from fraud, willful misconduct, knowing violation of law, bad faith, unauthorized self-dealing, gross negligence, breach of fiduciary duty, misappropriation, or material breach.

## Article 22 — Miscellaneous

### 22.1 Entire Agreement

This Agreement constitutes the entire agreement among the Members concerning the Company and supersedes all prior or contemporaneous oral or written agreements on the same subject.

### 22.2 Severability

If any provision is held invalid or unenforceable, the remaining provisions remain in effect. The invalid provision shall be modified to the minimum extent necessary to make it enforceable while preserving the parties' intent.

### 22.3 Waiver

Failure to enforce a provision is not a waiver. A waiver is effective only if in writing and signed by the waiving party.

### 22.4 Remedies

The Members acknowledge that breach involving Company Property, confidential information, credentials, customer data, supplier relationships, source code, domains, or platform control may cause irreparable harm. The Company and non-breaching Member may seek injunctive relief in addition to all other remedies.

### 22.5 Attorney Fees

In any dispute arising from this Agreement, the prevailing party may recover reasonable attorney fees, expert fees, costs, and expenses to the extent awarded by a court, arbitrator, or settlement.

### 22.6 No Third-Party Beneficiaries

This Agreement is for the benefit of the Company and Members only and creates no rights in third parties except as expressly provided.

### 22.7 Binding Effect

This Agreement binds and benefits the Members and their permitted heirs, successors, assigns, representatives, and transferees.

# Schedule E — Deadlock and Buy-Sell Procedure

> This schedule must be finalized by counsel before execution. The Members should select one mechanism and delete the alternatives not used.

## Option 1 — Appraised-Value Buyout

1. Either Member may trigger buyout after unresolved deadlock, dissociation, unauthorized withdrawal, or other trigger stated in this Agreement.
2. The purchase price shall be determined by an independent business appraiser selected by mutual agreement.
3. If the Members cannot agree on an appraiser within ten business days, each Member shall appoint one appraiser, and the two appraisers shall appoint a third. The third appraiser's valuation shall control unless the Members agree otherwise.
4. The valuation shall account for assets, liabilities, inventory, receivables, debt, IP, catalog data, platform assets, goodwill, customer data, supplier relationships, pending disputes, tax liabilities, working capital, and normalized earnings.
5. Payment shall be made [lump sum / installments over X months] with interest at [rate].

## Option 2 — Shotgun Buy-Sell

1. Either Member may offer to buy the other Member's entire interest for a stated price and terms.
2. The receiving Member must either sell on those terms or buy the offering Member's interest on the same economic terms.
3. Closing shall occur within [X] days unless extended by written agreement.

## Option 3 — Texas Shoot-Out / Sealed Bid

1. Each Member submits a sealed bid stating the price at which that Member would buy the other Member's interest.
2. The higher bidder buys the lower bidder's interest at the higher bid price or another formula approved by counsel.
3. Closing occurs within [X] days.

## Required Closing Terms

Any buyout must include:

1. transfer of accounts and credentials;
2. resignation from governance rights;
3. release of claims, subject to fraud and concealed liabilities exceptions;
4. confidentiality reaffirmation;
5. return of Company Property;
6. non-disparagement, if enforceable;
7. transition assistance;
8. tax allocation agreement;
9. treatment of personal guarantees; and
10. indemnity for pre-closing misconduct.
